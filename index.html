DEVELOPERS

Study Questions

  1. Which desktop computer has the highest processing performance?
    AnswerA High-End tier build — Ryzen 9 9950X3D paired with an RTX 5090 — has the highest processing performance in our lineup. The 16-core/32-thread CPU with 3D V-Cache and the 32GB GDDR7 flagship GPU together deliver the most raw compute, rendering, and 4K gaming power of any combination we sell.
  2. Which computer is most suitable for office work?
    AnswerA Budget tier build is most suitable for office work — a Ryzen 5 5500 CPU, 16GB of RAM, and a 500GB SSD comfortably handle browsing, documents, spreadsheets, and video calls without paying for gaming-grade power that office tasks never use.
  3. Which computer is recommended for gaming or graphic design?
    AnswerA Mid-Range build (Ryzen 7 7700 + RTX 5070 + 32GB RAM) is our go-to recommendation for gaming or graphic design — it handles modern AAA titles at 1440p and creative software smoothly. For 4K gaming, competitive esports, or professional 3D/video work, the High-End tier with the RTX 5090 is the step up.
  4. Which desktop offers the best value for a student?
    AnswerThe Mid-Range tier offers the best value for a student — parts like the Ryzen 7 7700, RTX 5070, and 32GB of RAM balance strong everyday and gaming performance with a price that won't strain a student budget, and leave headroom for schoolwork, research, and creative projects alike.
  5. Which component contributes the most to overall system performance? Explain your answer.
    AnswerThe CPU and GPU contribute the most, and which one matters more depends on the task. The CPU sets the ceiling for everyday responsiveness, multitasking, and any workload that relies on single or multi-core speed (office work, compiling, streaming). The GPU is what drives gaming frame rates, ray tracing, video rendering, and AI workloads. A system is only as fast as its weakest major component, so the two should be balanced — pairing a flagship GPU with a weak CPU (or vice versa) creates a bottleneck that wastes the stronger part's potential.
